The Ballycateen Sofa

Shown here in a dyed Irish linen, single feather and down seat cushion and exposed show wood in stained oak.

The sofa design from the Georgian Regency period.

Both the frame making and upholstery are carried out within a 20 kilometre radius of our base in Kinsdale Co. Cork. We use traditional methods and materials, which are carefully and sympathetically balanced with modern techniques and equipment. This allows us to keep the build of each sofa as true to the original as possible.

Features such as tenon joints and the smooth, hand shaped frame are characteristics we’ve been keen to carry forward from some of the most respected and well known sofa and chair makers of the 19th and 20th centuries.

The Upholstery

The Ballycatteen is upholstered in Kinsale using a careful blend of traditional methods and materials, as well as modern techniques and equipment where appropriate. We follow a build up process which is in-keeping with the original design, however all parameters can be adjusted to ensure maximum comfort and suitability to the customer.

Options:

  • The seat cushion can be supplied as a single, or as two or three separate cushions.
  • Show wood available in either oak, mahogany or ash.

The Upholstery

The Desmond sofa is upholstered in Kinsale using a careful blend of traditional methods and materials, as well as modern techniques and equipment where appropriate. We follow a build up process which is in-keeping with the original design, however all parameters can be adjusted to ensure maximum comfort and suitability to the customer.

A variation on the original Edwardian design is the subtle lift in the lumber springs to allow the insertion of a feather seat cushion. This minor change enhances both the look of the Desmond sofa as well as increasing the level of comfort.

The second alteration was the removal of the integral arm rests at the frame making stage, opting now for a smooth and elegant curve which continues down into the frame base.
